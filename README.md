# Muhammad Bilal – PhD Candidate in Civil Engineering

Welcome to my portfolio! I am a PhD candidate at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, specializing in geotechnical failure mechanisms, rainfall-induced landslides, and multi-hazard dynamics. I am passionate about combining numerical simulation, physical testing, and field investigation to advance landslide hazard assessment and early warning systems.

---

## 📌 About

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Civil Engineering at **Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU)** under the prestigious **Chinese Scholarship Council (CSC) Scholarship**. I hold a Master’s degree from **Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT)** and a Bachelor’s from **University of Engineering & Technology (UET) Taxila, Pakistan**.

My research focuses on:
- **Phase transition mechanisms** of clustered shallow flow-slide disaster chains under rainfall and freeze-thaw cycles.
- **Numerical modeling** of slope stability and large deformation failures using Abaqus, LPF3D, and GIS-based tools.
- **Early warning systems** for rainfall-induced landslides in southeastern China (National Key Project).

I have led multiple research projects, including the analysis of the **Xinyi Village landslide** under freeze-thaw cycles and the development of **predictive models for subgrade soil strength** in Pakistan. I am the lead author of a peer-reviewed paper in *Transportation Infrastructure Geotechnology* and co-author of several others in journals like *Engineering Geology* and *Journal of Road Engineering*.

Beyond research, I enjoy mentoring students (led a team of 6 in my final year project) and continuously expanding my skills in computational mechanics and data analysis.

## 🧪 Projects

### 🔬 Current PhD Project
**Research on the Phase Transition Mechanism of Clustered Shallow Flow-slide Disaster Chains in Southeastern Mountainous Areas**  
- National Key Project: *Dynamic Process and Hazard Assessment of a Complex Chain of Rainfall-Induced Landslides and Flows in Southeast China*  
- Tools: LPF3D, Abaqus, GIS  
- Goal: Understand cluster landslide behavior and improve early warning.

### ❄️ Master's Project
**Slope Stability Analysis under Freeze-Thaw Cycles**  
- Case study: Xinyi Village landslide, Northeast China  
- Methods: FEM using Abaqus, shear strength reduction analysis  
- Outcome: Revealed failure mechanisms under repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

### 🛣️ Bachelor's Project
**Prediction of Strength Characteristics from Index Properties for Indigenous Subgrade Soils (Potohar Region, Pakistan)**  
- Team lead (6 members)  
- Developed a correlation model for CBR prediction from UCC values  
- Published conference paper: *Regression Model for Predicting Soaked CBR from UCC* (CSCE 2021).
